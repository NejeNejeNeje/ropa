## What ROPA Does â€” The Full Tour

ROPA is like **Tinder, but for clothes**. Travelers post clothing they want to swap or sell, swipe through what others have posted, and when two people both like each other's stuff â€” it's a Match! They can chat, meet up, and trade.

Here is every feature that's already built and working:

### For Your Users (Travelers)

**Swipe Feed** â€” The main experience. Users browse clothing items one at a time, like a dating app. Swipe right if they like it, left to pass. When two people both swipe right on each other's items, the app creates a Match and opens a private chat between them.

**Offers and Negotiation** â€” When someone sees an item with a price tag, they can make a monetary offer. The seller can accept, decline, or counter-propose a different price. There's even a built-in "lowball protection" â€” if someone offers way below the asking price, it's automatically declined.

**Private Chat** â€” Once two users are matched, they can send each other messages, share photos, and coordinate where and when to swap. The chat has read receipts so you can see when your message has been read.

**Meetup Coordination** â€” Users can propose a meeting location, date, and time right inside the chat. The other person confirms, and both parties get the details. No need for WhatsApp or text messages.

**Drop Zones** â€” These are real physical locations (hostels, cafes, coworking spaces) that have a ROPA clothing shelf. Travelers can scan a QR code at the location to instantly list or claim items. This is where online meets offline â€” and it's one of your revenue streams.

**Swap Circles** â€” In-person community swap events where travelers gather to trade clothes face-to-face. Each event has a date, venue, capacity, and RSVP system. Users sign up directly in the app. You create and manage these from the admin dashboard.

**Community Feed** â€” Like a mini Instagram built into the app. Travelers post photos of their outfits with their swapped clothes, tag the city they're in, and others can like and share. This creates organic content that attracts new users.

**TravelSwap** â€” A bulletin board where users post: "I'm in Barcelona, heading to Zurich, I need a winter jacket, I have sandals to offer." The app automatically finds mutual matches â€” people who have what you need AND need what you have.

**Karma and Trust System** â€” Every good action (completing a swap, getting a 5-star review, posting to the community) earns karma points. Users level up from Bronze to Silver to Gold. Higher trust means better visibility and priority in the app.

**Profile and Travel Passport** â€” Users have a profile showing their karma level, cities they've visited, their style preferences, and their "Swap Buddies" â€” people they've successfully traded with before.

## How You Make Money â€” 4 Revenue Engines

ROPA was built with four ways to generate revenue, all ready to be activated:

### 1. Transaction Fees (2â€“5%)
Every time a user pays money for an item (not a free swap), you collect a small percentage. This is activated by connecting a Stripe account â€” the code is already written, it just needs your bank details plugged in.

### 2. ROPA Passport ($4.99/month)
A premium subscription that gives users instant Gold status, boosted listings in the feed, and access to exclusive Swap Circles. This turns your most active users into paying subscribers.

### 3. Drop Zone Partnerships
You charge hostels and cafes a monthly fee to be listed as an official ROPA Zone. In return, they get foot traffic from ROPA users looking to swap clothes. This is your B2B revenue stream. Target: backpacker hostels, coworking spaces, and boutique cafes in travel hub cities.

### 4. Sponsored Swap Circles
When you organize a Swap Circle event, brands like Patagonia, local vintage shops, or sustainable fashion labels can sponsor it. They pay for visibility, you host the event, the community benefits.
